Where does primary energy go — power plants or direct use?

Each primary source splits into electricity generation and direct non-electric consumption (heating, transport, industry). All values in terawatt-hours (TWh).

Reading the chart: the upper right node collects every source's electricity output; the lower right node collects the remainder (consumption − electricity generated). Method: non-fossil primary energy follows OWID's substitution method, so the “direct” residual of nuclear, hydro, wind and solar mostly reflects implied conversion losses rather than true end use; for fossil fuels it also includes power-plant losses.
Source: Our World in Data — Energy dataset (Energy Institute · Statistical Review of World Energy; Ember).
